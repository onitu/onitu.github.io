Open Source

The Onitu team made the project completely open source. Not only end users will be able to make sure nothing fishy is going on with their data, but the most tech savvy of them will potentially be able to put their code up for reviews, to propose new features for their favorite files sharing software.

Secure

Your files are never stored on any third party server: only you have access to your data, at any time, and safely. Using cryptography and the most efficient data encryption algorithms, Onitu makes sure nobody can eavesdrop on your connection while you access your files, or access them with a compromised account.

Free
  

All the features of Onitu are available for free, on the most popular systems out there. If you're using a Linux, Windows, or Mac powered computer, just download the appropriate package for your distribution and you are ready to go!

 EPITECH Innovative Projects

The Epitech Innovative Project (E.I.P.) is the main step in the curiculuum of students of Epitech that shift their status from mere student to the one of a full-fledged professional. Over the course of the 4th and 5th years, students have to take on an entire project and do everything that's required for it to be usable by professionals.

The goal of the E.I.P. is two folds:

  • it allows students to gain experience, by managing an entire project
  • it helps them to build up a high level reputation in the professional world

 The Onitu project

Onitu allows you to store your personal files, synchronize them and have access to them without limitations, wherever you are. Open source, it is a simple and secure application that gives you full control over your data. Usable without any prior training, it allows end users not to rely on a single company and actually choose how you wish to manage your files, by fine tuning the synchronization options of the configuration.

Onitu provides its users with a way to gather documents from the biggest files storage solutions out there, from Dropbox to Ubuntu One, including Amazon or even a local storage space. Designed to be scalable, by adding plugins to it, it makes adding at any given moment new storage solutions easy.

Finally, it is compatible with the Ubuntu One service, is perfectly embeddable in pre-existing environments, and will benefit as soon as it gets out from a large display of potential users.
